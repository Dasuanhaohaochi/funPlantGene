## Germplasm Phenotype:
   - [tin]()&nbsp;---&nbsp;The tin mutant was slightly shorter than the wild type when grown on the control medium, but maintained normal root and hypocotyl growth on turanose. Turanose mimics sucrose in its ability to stimulate adventitious  roots on wild-type hypocotyls. In the tin  mutant the number of adventitious roots induced by turanose and sucrose was clearly lower. Adult tin plants did not show any apparent orphological differences from the wild type, but their flowering time was delayed due to a prolonged vegetative phase. tin overproduced ethylene on the control medium; and sucrose, but not turanose, enhanced ethylene production. The control tin, and the sucrose-treated tin mutant, displays a higher IAA-conjugate level when compared with the wild type.
